Unraveling the Reasoning Core: Unique Tokens Drive Effective Reinforcement Learning for LLM Exploration

📖 Abstract

Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) has significantly advanced Large Language Model (LLM) reasoning; however, it faces a fundamental optimization instability: uniform token updates precipitate entropy collapse (premature convergence), while scalar entropy maximization risks entropy explosion (blind exploration).

To resolve this dichotomy, we introduce the Independent Combinatorial Tokens (ICT) framework, which shifts the focus from scalar uncertainty to the structural properties of logits distributions. By leveraging Jensen-Shannon (JS) divergence, ICT identifies unique tokens—critical branching points—to guide effective exploration.

Our theoretical analysis, grounded in second-order Rényi entropy, proves that selectively updating these tokens regulates strategy purity, effectively stabilizing the training landscape. Empirical results demonstrate that updating merely the top 10% of unique tokens yields a 5.65% improvement on the Math benchmark over full-parameter baselines, validating that structural information selection is essential for efficient reasoning alignment.

📖 Motivation

1. Limitations of General RLVR

Prevailing RLVR methods, such as GRPO, apply training signals uniformly across all tokens. This often triggers Entropy Collapse, where the model converges prematurely to local optima, resulting in insufficient exploration capacity.

2. Entropy Explosion & Metric Ambiguity

While existing works attempt to enhance exploration via entropy maximization, they frequently suffer from Entropy Explosion (persistent instability). Furthermore, as shown in Fig 1(a), scalar entropy is an ambiguous metric: identical entropy values can mask vastly different distribution structures, leading the model into "blind exploration" and reasoning dead ends.

3. Finding the Reasoning Core via Structure

To resolve this, we shift the focus from scalar uncertainty to logit distribution structures. By identifying Unique Tokens—the critical branching points of reasoning—our ICT framework enables Guided Exploration (Fig 1b), effectively stabilizing the training landscape while driving efficient reasoning alignment.

Key Insight: Instead of indiscriminate entropy control, targeting tokens with unique structural information is the key to balancing exploration and convergence in LLM reasoning.

🏗️ Methodology Details

The ICT framework represents a paradigm shift in RLVR by moving away from scalar uncertainty metrics. Instead, it focuses on the structural properties of logit distributions, enabling a significantly more precise selection of "Unique Tokens" for the optimization process.

🔹 Structural Selector

The framework employs Jensen-Shannon (JS) Divergence to quantify the statistical "uniqueness" of a token relative to the group consensus. This process isolates critical branching points—pivotal decision moments where the model's inference trajectories truly diverge—distinguishing them from high-entropy noise.

🔹 Sparse-GRPO Estimator

We introduce a binary sparse mask ($M_{i,t}$) designed to activate updates exclusively for tokens in the top $k$-percentile of uniqueness. This sparse update strategy effectively filters out redundant reasoning steps and stabilizes the gradient flow by ignoring low-information "trivial" tokens.

🔹 Optimization Stability

By regulating Strategy Purity, the framework resolves the optimization dichotomy in RLVR. It prevents "Entropy Collapse" (premature convergence) while simultaneously avoiding "Entropy Explosion" (blind exploration), resulting in a 5.65% improvement on the Math benchmark.

📈 Analysis of Optimal Sparsity

These figures collectively demonstrate why 10% is the optimal threshold for identifying the "Reasoning Core" of an LLM.

Less is More

Figure 3 compares reward trajectories across different ratios. The 10% strategy (orange) achieves the highest stable reward, outperforming the full uniform update (orange) and larger sparse ratios (20-30%). Notably, updating the remaining 90% (purple) yields the lowest results, proving those tokens provide negligible value for reasoning.

Empirical Validation

Figure 4 illustrates the sorted JS Divergence scores for the Qwen2.5-1.5B model. We observe a sharp "Elbow Effect" (red dashed line) at the top 10% percentile. This indicates that while most tokens share redundant structures, only this top 10%—the Unique Tokens—represent critical branching points in reasoning.

Key Insight: Less is More. Targeting the structural core (top 10%) is more efficient and effective than dense, uniform updates.

⚙️ Robustness & Token Properties

1. Warm-up Independence

A critical requirement for any reinforcement learning framework is its temporal robustness. As demonstrated in the evaluation of intervention timing, the ICT framework maintains exceptional stability regardless of when the sparse update logic is introduced. Whether the transition from dense to sparse training begins early (step 10) or late (step 40), all trajectories converge to a consistent reward plateau. This proves that the method is not sensitive to initial policy noise and can effectively stabilize the training landscape across various stages of model development.

2. Structural vs. Uncertainty

Our analysis further reveals a fundamental distinction between structural uniqueness and scalar uncertainty. While traditional exploration strategies prioritize high-entropy tokens, the ICT framework identifies critical decision hubs that are often overlooked. The evidence shows an approximately 1:1 ratio between high-entropy and low-entropy tokens among the identified "unique" sets. This indicates that even tokens with high model confidence can be essential branching points for logical reasoning. By targeting these structural markers, the model preserves strategic purity while avoiding the chaos of blind exploration.
